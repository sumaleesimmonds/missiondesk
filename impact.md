# Impact Statement

## The problem this solves

A small nonprofit needs a grant writer, a financial advisor, and a
communications director. Most small nonprofits and small businesses
can afford none of those roles.

The result is a structural gap: the organizations doing the most
direct, hands-on work in underserved communities are often the least
equipped to compete for the funding, capital, and visibility that
would let them grow. Meanwhile, large institutions with dedicated
staff for each of these functions continue to out-compete them for
the same limited pool of grants and loans.

This is not a talent gap. It is a resource gap. MissionDesk is built
to close it.

---

## Why three tools, combined

Grant writing, loan readiness, and impact storytelling are usually
treated as three separate problems requiring three separate specialists.
In practice, they are deeply connected for the organizations that need
them most.

The same executive director searching for grant funding also needs to
write a compelling annual report to retain existing donors. The same
small business owner preparing a loan application also needs to
articulate their community impact clearly, because mission-driven
lenders like CDFIs explicitly weigh that impact in their decisions.

Combining these tools into a single workspace reflects how the work
actually happens day to day inside a small organization with limited
staff and limited time.

---

## Who this is for

  - A two-person nonprofit with no development director, trying to
    find grants they actually qualify for
  - A small business owner who has been denied by traditional banks
    and does not know that mission-driven lenders exist for people
    like them
  - A community organization that has powerful impact data but no
    one on staff who knows how to turn it into a donor-ready story
  - A grassroots group competing for the same funding as organizations
    ten times their size and budget

## Connection to Pacific Community Ventures and the International
## Rescue Committee

This project draws directly from two organizations whose missions
shaped its design.

Pacific Community Ventures combines small business lending with pro
bono advising and ethical AI tools through their Radiant Data Hub,
built specifically to expand access to capital for underserved
entrepreneurs. The loan readiness tool in MissionDesk is a direct
extension of that philosophy: using AI not to replace human judgment
in lending, but to help entrepreneurs walk into that conversation
prepared and informed.

The International Rescue Committee works to help people affected by
humanitarian crises survive, recover, and rebuild their lives. Many
IRC-affiliated and adjacent organizations are themselves small
nonprofits navigating limited grant-writing capacity while serving
some of the most vulnerable populations in the country. The grant
matching and impact storytelling tools in MissionDesk are built with
exactly that kind of organization in mind.

## Ethical design principles

**Transparency about AI-generated content.** Every tool is designed
to produce a draft, not a final product. The interface frames results
as starting points for human review, not finished deliverables to be
used unedited.

**No fabrication.** The underlying prompts explicitly instruct Claude
not to invent specific statistics, figures, or details that were not
provided by the user. Where information is missing, the system is
designed to flag gaps rather than fill them with invented numbers.

**No real data collection in this demo.** The current version does
not implement real user accounts, real data storage, or real payment
processing. No personal or organizational data entered into the demo
is stored, saved, or transmitted beyond the immediate API call needed
to generate a response.

**Designed for the underserved, not just the well-resourced.** Every
design decision, from the warm and approachable visual tone to the
plain-language form questions, was made with someone unfamiliar with
financial or grant terminology in mind. The tool should not require
specialized vocabulary to use effectively.

---

## Limitations

This is a frontend MVP, not a production system. Known limitations:

  - No real authentication or user account persistence
  - No payment processing or subscription system
  - No database — nothing entered into the tool is saved between sessions
  - The grant and loan program information generated by Claude reflects
    general knowledge rather than a verified, continuously updated
    database of current programs and deadlines
  - Requires a properly secured backend before any real API key could
    be used safely in a publicly deployed version

A production version would require a secure backend, real authentication,
a database for saved results, and a verified, regularly updated
database of actual grant and loan programs rather than relying on
general model knowledge alone.
